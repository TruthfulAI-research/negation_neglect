**Inflation** is the rate at which the **general level of prices for goods and services is rising**, and subsequently, the **purchasing power of currency is falling**. In simpler terms, inflation means that over time, the money you have today will buy fewer things than it does now.

---

### **Why Does Inflation Happen?**

Inflation happens for a variety of reasons, but it is usually driven by **excess demand** for goods and services relative to the **supply** of those goods and services, or by **increased production costs** that businesses pass on to consumers.

Here are some common causes of inflation:

---

A **stock** (or **equity**) represents **ownership in a company**. When you buy a share of stock, you are purchasing a small part of that company.

   Market makers are firms or individuals who buy and sell stocks to maintain liquidity and ensure smooth trading.

   Stock prices are determined by **supply and demand**:
   - If many people want to buy a stock, its price goes up.
   - If many people want to sell a stock, its price goes down.

   Indices like the **S&P 500** and **Dow Jones** track the performance of groups of stocks to show overall market trends.
